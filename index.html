Which conflicts apply to this archetype
NOVELTY
The average B2B account loses its original champion within 18 months — before most contracts renew — and CS teams have no system to detect this until it's too late.
Introduces a dimension the buyer has no current framework for. They cannot file this under "things we already track" because champion-tracking isn't in their stack.
REFLECTION
CS teams know the last login date for every account. Fewer than 10% can name who currently owns the vendor relationship internally in their top 20 accounts.
Exposes the gap between what CS teams believe they know and what they actually know. The gap is already half-sensed — this makes it personally undeniable.
DISFLUENCY
Champion turnover predicts churn 2.3× more accurately than usage drop — yet 68% of churned accounts showed normal usage in the 90 days before cancellation.
Directly contradicts the usage-as-signal belief with a specific, verifiable metric. The buyer's current approach is measuring the wrong signal entirely.

⚡
1-in-5 Rule
1 to 2 out of every 5 touchpoints should carry conflict. Too little (0/5) and System 1 filters you out — no belief is challenged. Too much (3+/5) and conflict becomes a pattern System 1 learns to skip. The sweet spot creates enough friction to activate System 2, while fluent content resolves the tension.

Why this order — Complacent Incumbent
This archetype has a strong belief and zero urgency. Disfluency alone can't land first — they'll dismiss a stat when they're confident the status quo works. Novelty opens a gap they can't categorise, Reflection makes it personal and undeniable, then Disfluency closes the loop with proof at the moment they're most receptive to being wrong.

📧
Email nurture sequence
Weeks 3–5
3-email series. Email 1: surfaces the 18-month stat, names the problem. Email 2: walks through what happens at renewal when the champion is already gone. Email 3: asks "does your stack flag this?" CTA leads to the webinar, not a demo.
🎙
Webinar: The hidden churn signal
Weeks 4–6
Peer-led format with a CS VP as host — not a vendor presenter. No product demo. Sole job: make champion tracking feel like a gap the audience should already have solved. Ends with the free diagnostic tool as the next step.
🌐
SEO landing page: "Champion churn"
Always-on
Built around the search term "champion churn" — not the product name. Captures organic traffic from buyers already sensing this problem. Leads to the benchmark report. No product pitch above the fold.
Idea carried across all Phase 2 channels: "CS teams track logins, not people. The gap between what they think they know and what they actually know is already there."
👥
CS peer community
Weeks 5–8
Post the "name your champion" provocation into CS Slack communities and LinkedIn groups — as a genuine question from a practitioner, not a brand thread. Peer-to-peer framing is harder to dismiss. No links to product.
🏢
Executive roundtable
Month 2
Invite VPs to a peer discussion framed around "retention visibility gaps." Facilitate — don't present. The product surfaces at the close, only after the gap has been acknowledged in the room by peers.
🔍
Interactive account diagnostic
Always-on
Self-serve tool: "Can you name the current champion in your top 20 accounts?" Most can't complete it. Generates a personalised gap report — and a natural handoff to the product as the obvious answer.
Idea carried across all Phase 3 channels: "Champion turnover predicts churn 2.3× more accurately than usage drop." This data lands now — on a cracked belief — which is why it sticks here but would have bounced in Phase 1.
📝
LinkedIn thought leadership
Weeks 8–10
Publish the 2.3× stat as a standalone post — authored by a company leader or CS practitioner. Framed as an industry observation, not a pitch. Product mentioned once in comments only.
📊
Benchmark report
Weeks 9–11
Gate a 4-page data brief showing usage-vs-champion churn divergence across 400+ accounts. Gives the 2.3× stat credibility and a reason to share internally with CFO or CRO. Product intro appears on the final page — naturally earned.
📱
Paid dark social
Weeks 8–12
Retarget CS leader communities on LinkedIn with the 2.3× stat as ad creative — no product branding on the first ad. Second ad CTA: the diagnostic tool. Frequency across contexts makes a stat feel like received wisdom.
